About Me

I started learning Chinese calligraphy when I was three years old, and there is something that has been with me for fifteen years. It witnessed my very first calligraphy class, the very first character I wrote, and even my very first little piece of work.

I used to practice it for at least one hour every day, whether at school or at home. Over time, calligraphy simply became part of my life. By the time I graduated from elementary school, I even held my own calligraphy exhibition and invited the principal to visit.

Calligraphy not only connects me with traditional Chinese culture, but also taught me persistence and focus.

Five Script Styles

Seal Script

Ancient and elegant, with rounded strokes and a strong sense of ritual form.

Seal Script

Its balanced structure and flowing lines express antiquity, dignity, and calm order.

Clerical Script

Recognisable for its broad strokes and sweeping finish, marking a key stage in character evolution.

Clerical Script

Its stretched form and measured rhythm create a strong sense of classical structure.

Cursive Script

Simplified in form and linked in motion, it is fast, expressive, and full of energy.

Cursive Script

It values spirit and speed, revealing emotion, rhythm, and movement most directly.

Running Script

Fluid and natural, it stands between regular script and cursive in both speed and clarity.

Running Script

Readable yet artistic, it is widely used for both everyday writing and personal expression.

Regular Script

Clear, upright, and carefully structured, it forms the foundation of standard character writing.

Regular Script

Its strict order and precision make it ideal for building discipline and technical control.
